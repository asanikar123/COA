Our Mission

Building a Just Community Through Creative Freedom

City of Asylum works at the intersection of human rights, literature, and community — three pillars that define everything we do.

🛡️

Protect

We provide a safe haven for writers (and families) living in exile under threat of persecution — a safe home, legal support, medical benefits, and the freedom to keep writing and publishing.

✨

Celebrate

Through free literary readings, concerts, films, and festivals, we celebrate the power of creative expression and build cultural bridges that connect people.

🏡

Transform

By transforming dilapidated properties into homes for writers and vibrant cultural spaces, we anchor neighborhood development on Pittsburgh's Northside — where art meets activism.

Our Story

Born from a Single Moment of Inspiration

In the 1990s, Diane Samuels and Henry Reese learned about the Cities of Asylum network in Europe — cities that protected writers whose lives were in danger. They were immediately drawn to this mission and saw how it could transform Pittsburgh.

They wrote to Cities of Asylum in Europe. They heard nothing. They kept writing for six years — until in 2003 they connected with author Russell Banks, who was expanding the network to the U.S.

In 2004, Huang Xiang arrived — a Chinese poet who had been Imprisoned for twelve years and tortured because of his writing. He painted his poetry in massive calligraphy across the facade of his new home on Sampsonia Way. That single act of creative defiance defined everything that came after at City of Asylum.

"You would expect people to run away, but they ran forward. It's a metaphor — the physical embodiment of what the City of Asylum community stands for." — Henry Reese, Co-Founder, on Chautauqua

Pittsburgh Is the Only Safe Haven Not Funded by Governments

Unlike every other city in the 90-city International Cities of Asylum Network, City of Asylum is entirely funded by individuals and foundations. Your donation helps protect a writer's life and freedom.

Our writers have received the Václav Havel Prize, the Four Freedoms Award, and nominations for Ukraine's highest literary honor. They don't need fame to deserve protection — but the world has noticed.
