Writers & Residencies

These are the voices the world tried to silence.

City of Asylum hosts the largest residency program in the world for writers living in exile under threat of persecution. For two years or more, we provide each writer with a home, a stipend, legal counsel, medical benefits, and the freedom to create.

The Exiled Writer & Artist Residency Program

Our residency is a long-term safe haven for literary writers and artists who are in exile from their home countries and under threat of persecution because of their work. The goal is to enable each writer to continue creating while transitioning to a stable, independent life in exile.

Our program is effective: every writer or artist hosted by City of Asylum has created a full-length work while in residence, and has been able to continue to create.

Safe Housing A home on Sampsonia Way, Pittsburgh's Northside
Living Stipend Financial support for two years or more
Legal Counsel Immigration and legal support for writers and families
Medical Benefits Healthcare for writers during their residency

Fellowship for Ukrainian Writers

In response to the 2022 invasion of Ukraine, City of Asylum launched a fellowship enabling three Ukrainian writers — Olena Boryshpolets, Oleksandr Frazé-Frazénko, and Volodymyr Rafeyenko — to come to Pittsburgh so they can continue to research, write, and publish despite the ongoing war.
