Writers & Residencies

These are the voices the world tried to silence.

City of Asylum hosts the largest residency program in the world for writers living in exile under threat of persecution. For three years or more, we provide each writer with a home, a stipend, legal counsel, medical benefits, and the freedom to create.

The Exiled Writer & Artist Residency Program

Our residency is a long-term safe haven for literary writers who are in exile from their home countries and under threat of persecution because of their work. The goal is to enable each writer to continue writing while transitioning to life in exile, if it is not safe for them to return to their home country.

Our program is effective: every writer or artist hosted by City of Asylum has created a full-length work while in residence, and has been able to continue to create.

Fellowship for Ukrainian Writers

In response to the 2022 invasion of Ukraine, City of Asylum launched a fellowship enabling three Ukrainian writers — Olena Boryshpolets, Oleksandr Frazé-Frazénko, and Volodymyr Rafeyenko — to come to Pittsburgh so they can continue to research, write, and publish despite the ongoing war.
